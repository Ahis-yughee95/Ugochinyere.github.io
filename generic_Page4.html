Maternal Health Data Analysis: Identifying Risk Factors and Predictive Insights

Introduction:
This study examines maternal health data to address critical issues in maternal morbidity and mortality. Focusing on systolic blood pressure (SBP) as a key indicator, the goal is to identify preventive actions and insights for managing high-risk conditions.

Analysis Overview

  • Linear Modeling: A linear regression model was developed to predict SBP using significant variables like age, blood sugar (BS), body temperature, and diastolic blood pressure (DBP), which were identified through hypothesis testing.
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA): PCA reduced data dimensions, with age and DBP as primary components explaining 63% of data variance, simplifying data while retaining key insights.
  • Age and Heart Rate Analysis: Age groups were classified per WHO standards, showing a gradual increase in heart rate with age, with older groups exhibiting higher average heart rates.
  • Association Mining: Association rules revealed co-occurrence of low, normal, and high SBP and DBP levels, indicating the likelihood of patients having similar levels of both pressures.
  • Clustering Analysis: KMeans clustering identified three patient clusters based on SBP, reflecting varying blood pressure risk levels, with older patients and those with higher DBP tending towards higher-risk clusters.
  • Correlation Analysis: A moderate positive correlation (0.42) was observed between age and SBP, aligning with literature that suggests age contributes to increased blood pressure in maternal health.

Predictive Modeling

The model can predict SBP using age, BS, body temperature, and DBP, enabling targeted monitoring for high-risk groups, particularly older women and those with elevated DBP.

Recommendations

  • Early Interventions: Regular BP monitoring and preventive actions are recommended for older and high-risk women to improve maternal health outcomes.
  • Further Research: Further research is suggested to refine predictive models and explore other risk factors associated with maternal health complications.

Conclusion

This analysis highlights the importance of BP monitoring and tailored interventions for high-risk maternal groups. It provides actionable insights for healthcare providers to reduce maternal mortality rates through data-driven health management strategies.
