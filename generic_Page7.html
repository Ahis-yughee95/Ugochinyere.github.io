Evaluation of Deep Learning Models for Bird Image Species Identification

Project Overview:
This project focused on developing an automated bird species classification system using deep learning, specifically targeting the identification of 100 bird species from a dataset containing over 84,000 images sourced from Kaggle. The evaluation included transfer learning models (InceptionResNetV2, MobileNetV2) and shallow CNN architectures (CS-CNNs) for bird species recognition.

Data Overview and Preprocessing

The dataset consisted of 84,635 training images and 2,625 test/validation images, representing birds from 100 species. To improve model generalization, augmentation techniques such as rotation, flipping, and zooming were applied to the images, ensuring a diverse dataset for training.

Model Architectures

The following models were evaluated:

  • Transfer Learning Models: InceptionResNetV2 and MobileNetV2 were fine-tuned on the bird dataset to leverage pre-trained features and improve performance on the classification task.
  • Constructed CNN Models: Two shallow CNN architectures were built: one with a 160x160 input size and another with 224x224 input size to compare the performance of different architectures.

Evaluation Metrics

The models were evaluated based on accuracy, with hyperparameter tuning and early stopping techniques applied to avoid overfitting and improve generalization performance.

Results

  • InceptionResNetV2: Achieved the highest performance, with 90% training accuracy, 86% validation accuracy, and 91% test accuracy.
  • MobileNetV2: Followed closely with 86% training accuracy and 83% test accuracy.
  • Shallow CNN Models: The 224x224 model achieved 73% test accuracy, while the 160x160 model performed below expectations with 61% test accuracy.

Conclusion

The project demonstrated that transfer learning models, particularly InceptionResNetV2, were highly effective for bird species identification. These models significantly outperformed shallow CNN architectures, providing a more accurate and efficient approach for automating bird species classification. This research offers a promising tool for biodiversity monitoring and conservation efforts, aiding in the classification of bird species with high accuracy.

Recommendations

  1. Adopt Transfer Learning Models: Transfer learning models like InceptionResNetV2 should be adopted for real-world bird species identification tasks due to their superior accuracy and efficiency.
  2. Focus on Image Augmentation: Continue applying data augmentation techniques to further enhance model generalization and robustness, especially when working with limited datasets.
  3. Explore Further Model Optimization: Explore other advanced CNN architectures or hybrid models to improve classification accuracy and reduce computational cost.
